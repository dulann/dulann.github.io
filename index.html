Intro

Dr. Lan Du is a senior lecturer at Monash University, and affiliated with the machine learning group in Department of Data Science and AI. He is a teaching and research academic in the Faculty of IT, Monash University. He is also faculty's director of master programs. Before joining Monash, he was a post-doc research fellow working with Prof Mark Johnson at Macquarie University. He recieved his Ph.D. degree in computer science from the Australian National University, under the supervision of Prof Wray Buntine and Dr. Huidong Jin.

His research interest lies in the joint area of machine/deep learning and natural language processing and their applications in different domians, such as public health, where he and his resarch team are developing cutting-edge NLP technologies for AI-enabled medical NLP.

Particularly, his research touches on

  • Deep generative modells
  • Deep Multimodal learning (with interpretability)
  • Multilabel learning for NLP and CV
  • Neural topic modelling
  • Nearest neigbour methods for computer vision
  • Knowledge distillation
  • Federated Learning
  • Meta Learning
